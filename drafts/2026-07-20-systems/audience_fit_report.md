## Story comparison

### Bookkeeping AI is moving into the workflow, not the side window

- Audience: small business owners, accountants, bookkeepers, and operations leads
- Interest signal: Xero said its JAX platform is adding AI-powered document capture and workflow automation and that it serves 5 million customers worldwide.
- Stop reason: The story becomes interesting when the workflow handoff is visible: AI prepares the draft, then a human approves the posting.
- Payoff: Shows whether AI can reduce repeatable bookkeeping steps while keeping finance control intact.
- Proof: Xero said its JAX agentic platform is adding AI-powered features for small businesses, accountants and bookkeepers, including document capture and workflow automation, and said it serves 5 million customers worldwide.
- Overall score: 8/10
- Reason: Very strong fit for small-business workflow audiences because the benefit is concrete, the process question is easy to understand, and the verified fact maps cleanly to a practical test.

### AI meeting notes are becoming an admin control

- Audience: team admins, managers, operations leaders, and small business operators
- Interest signal: Google said admins can configure Meet AI note-taking so it only applies to meetings with three or more people.
- Stop reason: The story turns once the setting is framed as a policy decision instead of a personal convenience feature.
- Payoff: Shows how to turn meeting AI into a standard operating rule with a clear review point.
- Proof: Google said admins can now configure AI note-taking in Meet so it can be enabled only for meetings with three or more people, with rollout and default-setting details by plan.
- Overall score: 8/10
- Reason: Excellent for workplace audiences because it is specific, practical, and easy to explain as a settings and ownership question.

### Notion is turning shared boards into a coordination layer

- Audience: Notion users, project managers, operations teams, and knowledge workers
- Interest signal: Notion said users can assign external agents such as Claude and Cursor from a shared board.
- Stop reason: The useful angle appears when the board becomes a place where tasks, files, and agent actions meet.
- Payoff: Shows whether one shared board can carry work from meeting note to follow-up without losing traceability.
- Proof: Notion said users can assign external agents such as Claude and Cursor from a shared board, AI Meeting Notes now include speaker labels, and agents can read and write more file types and connect to more tools.
- Overall score: 8/10
- Reason: Strong coordination-workflow story with good novelty and clear operational implications, especially for Notion users and team leads.

### Support AI is being sold like a service, not a project

- Audience: support leaders, SMB owners, service managers, and customer operations teams
- Interest signal: Salesforce said Agentforce Help Agent and Agentforce Customer Service Portal will be generally available in July 2026 and that pay-per-resolution pricing will be available then too.
- Stop reason: The key question is no longer only capability; it is how the pricing and packaging change the operating decision.
- Payoff: Helps leaders compare the cost of a resolved case against the time their queue currently consumes.
- Proof: Salesforce said Agentforce Help Agent and Agentforce Customer Service Portal are generally available in July 2026 and that pay-per-resolution pricing will be available then too; it also said it has signed an agreement to acquire Fin, a customer-agent platform for SMBs.
- Overall score: 8/10
- Reason: Best for support and SMB audiences because the pricing model creates a direct operational decision and the verified facts are easy to translate into a practical test.
