## Story comparison

### ChatGPT is becoming easier to search and easier to reach

- Audience: People who use ChatGPT for drafts, notes, plans, small-business work, or study materials and want to find past work faster
- Interest signal: OpenAI says ChatGPT can search across chats, projects, images and documents, and it is back on WhatsApp in the EEA
- Stop reason: This fixes a familiar pain point: finding old work without hunting through separate places
- Payoff: A searchable working archive that makes it easier to pick up where you left off
- Proof: OpenAI says ChatGPT can now search across chats, projects, images and documents on web, iOS and Android, and is available again on WhatsApp in the EEA with voice notes, image uploads and image generation
- Overall score: 8/10
- Reason: Very clear everyday use case with strong search interest and immediate practical value; slightly less visually dramatic than browser automation.

### Google is turning research notes into a connected study workspace

- Audience: Students, self-learners, researchers, and people who keep notes, trip plans, or project docs in multiple places
- Interest signal: NotebookLM is now Gemini Notebook with a secure cloud computer, native code execution, and syncing across Gemini app and Google Search
- Stop reason: It turns a research tool into a broader workspace, which is useful but slightly less instantly relatable than study or browser workflows
- Payoff: Less duplicate copying and less lost context when organising information
- Proof: Google says NotebookLM has been renamed Gemini Notebook, now has a secure cloud computer, can run code natively, and is syncing notebooks across the Gemini app and Google Search
- Overall score: 7/10
- Reason: Strong for organisation-minded users and clear within Google's ecosystem, but the value is more workflow-specific than broad.

### Study notebooks are a clearer use case than generic chat for learners

- Audience: Students, parents, and self-learners who need structure for revision and practice
- Interest signal: Google says study notebooks create diagnostic quizzes, bite-sized lessons, practice quizzes, and a progress dashboard
- Stop reason: The learner payoff is immediately understandable: structure, practice, and progress tracking
- Payoff: A guided study system that shows what to do next instead of only answering questions
- Proof: Google says study notebooks generate diagnostic quizzes, personalised bite-sized lessons, practice quizzes and a progress dashboard, and they are rolling out globally for personal accounts and soon for school-issued accounts
- Overall score: 8/10
- Reason: Very easy to explain and highly useful for learners, with a concrete structure that translates well into advice content.

### Chrome is becoming a practical admin assistant in the browser

- Audience: People who do a lot of web research, email coordination, scheduling, or tab-heavy planning at work
- Interest signal: Gemini in Chrome can summarise pages, compare tabs, draft Gmail messages, schedule Calendar events, check Maps details, and answer questions about YouTube videos
- Stop reason: It speaks to a common work frustration and shows up in a place people already use all day
- Payoff: More admin handled inside the browser, with less switching and less rework
- Proof: Google says Gemini in Chrome started rolling out to desktop users in the U.K. on July 14, 2026, with iOS next month, and can summarise pages, compare tabs, draft emails, schedule meetings, and ask questions about YouTube videos
- Overall score: 9/10
- Reason: Best mix of everyday utility, workplace relevance, and easy demonstration; strong fit for action-oriented audiences.
