# Clearforge Audience-Fit Report — 2026-07-23

## Story comparison

### Google Search is becoming a task hub, not just a query box

- Audience: Creators, solo operators, and small businesses that want fewer app hops in everyday planning and publishing
- Interest signal: Search is starting to hand work into other apps instead of only pointing to information
- Stop reason: App-switching friction is familiar and easy to recognise
- Payoff: One prompt can start a task and pass it into the right app
- Proof: Google says users can securely link apps such as Instacart, Canva and YouTube Music directly to AI Mode in Search, with rollout starting in the U.S. this week
- Overall score: 8/10
- Reason: Clear everyday value, easy to explain fast, and strongly searchable because it ties AI Search to familiar apps and task switching.

### Google Vids now covers more of the video pipeline

- Audience: Creators, marketers, trainers, and small teams that need quick video output with a visible AI workflow
- Interest signal: Video creation is moving into a single prompt-and-edit workflow with avatars and watermarks
- Stop reason: The workflow is visually intuitive and easy to picture in one screen
- Payoff: Draft, revise and deliver short video content inside one tool
- Proof: Google says Gemini Omni and personal avatars are now available in Google Vids for eligible Google AI Pro, Ultra and Workspace business customers, with SynthID watermarks on generated clips
- Overall score: 7/10
- Reason: Strong visual story with practical usefulness for small teams, though slightly narrower and less discussion-heavy than the workflow stories.

### Jira is being redesigned around agent handoffs

- Audience: Product, engineering and project teams that already rely on Slack, Jira, Confluence and GitHub
- Interest signal: Atlassian is treating AI as part of the governed workflow stack, not a separate coding add-on
- Stop reason: The pain point is operational and common in team environments
- Payoff: Conversation can become structured work while human review stays in the loop
- Proof: Atlassian says Jira Planner and Jira for Slack are designed to turn context from Jira, Confluence, Slack and GitHub into structured work for agents, with human review kept in the loop
- Overall score: 8/10
- Reason: Best mix of real operational consequence, clear workflow language and strong appeal to teams already using the tools named in the source.

### Zoom is targeting regulated workflows with on-prem AI

- Audience: IT, security, compliance and operations teams in regulated organisations and sensitive workplaces
- Interest signal: Zoom is offering AI that can run on customer infrastructure or private cloud
- Stop reason: Data residency and governance are high-stakes, easy-to-understand concerns
- Payoff: Meeting AI can stay inside existing control boundaries
- Proof: Zoom says AI On-Prem is available through Zoom Node add-on to paid Zoom Workplace plans and can run AI workloads on customer infrastructure or private cloud
- Overall score: 8/10
- Reason: High-consequence story for regulated teams, with a clear decision angle around deployment and data handling rather than model quality.

## Overall reasoning

The strongest overall pattern is workflow integration rather than standalone model capability. Story 0 is the broadest and easiest to explain for consumers and creators, story 2 is the best operational story for teams, story 1 is the clearest visual creator story, and story 3 is the most important for regulated environments. Each platform selection is matched to the kind of question that audience is most likely to answer.
