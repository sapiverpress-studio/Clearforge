## Genuine audience problem

### AI creates drafts faster than teams can finish them

Solo creators and small businesses can now use AI to draft, summarize, and analyze quickly, but the recurring bottleneck is the finishing work: checking facts, tracking what the model touched, adding disclosure, and getting a human sign-off before anything customer-facing, public, or money-related goes live.

- **Affected audience:** Solo creators, freelancers, small agencies, and small businesses using AI for content, support, research, ads, or internal ops.
- **Practical consequence:** The time saved by AI gets eaten by cleanup and uncertainty; weak review can put incorrect, undisclosed, or unsafe output into public workflows; teams need a simple repeatable finish-line check.
- **Why now:** Across this week’s approved research, the focus keeps shifting from model capability to proof and controls: OpenAI asks buyers to measure useful work completed, GPT-Red hardens systems against prompt injection, Google adds ad disclosure, GitHub puts AI into pull-request review, OpenAI adds age prediction and family controls, and multiple sources emphasize observability and approval gates.
- **Evergreen value:** Any workflow that uses AI will keep needing a way to decide whether the output is ready, who approves it, and what gets logged before it ships.
- **Confidence:** high

## Recommended product

### AI Finish-Line Scorecard

**Promise:** Help solo creators and small businesses decide whether AI output is actually ready to ship.

**Target customer:** Solo creators, freelancers, and small businesses using AI for drafts, support replies, research summaries, ads, or internal operations.

**Outcome:** A repeatable weekly process for measuring time saved, cleanup required, disclosure needs, and human approval before anything goes live.

**Minimum viable contents:**
- 1-page finish-line scorecard
- approval-gate checklist
- disclosure and provenance log
- rollback note template
- one-week workflow test sheet

- **Worked example:** A solo consultant drafts a client recap with AI, then uses the scorecard to record source notes, edit count, disclosure need, and sign-off before sending it.
- **Suggested price:** £0
- **Main objection:** People can build this themselves in a note or spreadsheet.
- **Demonstration:** Show the same workflow twice — once without the scorecard and once with it — so users can compare cleanup time, approval steps, and missing disclosure before the output ships.
- **Bundle destination:** Clearforge free library / lead magnet

## Editorial guardrails

- Do not claim the resource will increase income or guarantee faster growth; frame it as a workflow check.
- Treat platform disclosure rules, age controls, and safety features as product-specific and subject to region or plan differences.
- Do not overstate demand for a paid pack; this week’s evidence supports a reusable free resource first.
- Use the tool only for operational review, not legal, medical, or financial advice.
